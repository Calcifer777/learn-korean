어느 추운 겨울밤이었어요. 배고픈 호랑이 한 마리가 먹을 것을 찾아 마을로 내려왔어요.
It was a cold winter night. A hungry tiger came down to the village to find something to eat.

“어흥, 배고파.” 그때 아이 울음소리가 들렸어요. “으앙, 으앙!” 
"Roar, I'm hungry." Just then, the sound of a child crying was heard. "Waah, waah!"

호랑이는 소리가 나는 집을 찾기 시작했어요. “오호, 저 집이구나!” 
The tiger began to look for the house where the sound was coming from. "Oho, it's that house!"

호랑이는 엄마와 아이가 있는 집에 도착했어요. 그리고 조용히 문 앞까지 다가갔어요.
The tiger arrived at the house where a mother and her child were. And he quietly approached the front door.

“으앙, 으앙!” 아이는 여전히 큰 소리로 울고 있었어요.
"Waah, waah!" The child was still crying loudly.

“얘야, 그만 울어. 계속 울면 도깨비가 와서 잡아갈 거야.”
"Hey, stop crying. If you keep crying, a goblin will come and take you away."

엄마의 말에 아이는 더 크게 “으앙!” 하고 울었어요.
At the mother's words, the child cried even louder, "Waah!"

아이의 울음소리를 들은 호랑이는 생각했어요. ‘도깨비를 안 무서워하네? 역시 이 호랑이 정도는 돼야 무섭지!’
Hearing the child's crying, the tiger thought: "He's not afraid of a goblin? As expected, it takes someone like this tiger to be scary!"

그때였어요. 아이 엄마가 아이에게 말했어요.
It was then. The child's mother said to the child:

“얘야, 그만 울어. 밖에 무서운 호랑이가 와 있어. 계속 울면 호랑이가 잡아갈 거야.”
"Hey, stop crying. There is a scary tiger outside. If you keep crying, the tiger will take you away."

‘아니, 내가 여기에 있는 걸 어떻게 알았지?’ 호랑이는 깜짝 놀랐어요.
"Wait, how did she know I was here?" The tiger was startled.

그런데도 아이는 여전히 울음을 그치지 않았어요. “으앙, 으앙!”
Even so, the child still did not stop crying. "Waah, waah!"

‘저 애는 내가 무섭지도 않나 봐.’ 호랑이는 당황했어요.
"That kid must not even be afraid of me." The tiger was flustered.

엄마가 아이에게 다시 말했어요. “얘야, 엄마가 곶감 줄게, 곶감.”
The mother spoke to the child again. "Hey, Mommy will give you a dried persimmon, a dried persimmon."

곶감이라는 말에 아이가 곧바로 울음을 그쳤어요.
At the mention of a dried persimmon, the child stopped crying immediately.

호랑이는 생각했어요. ‘곶감이라는 놈이 누구길래 이렇게 곧바로 울음을 그치지? 곶감은 나보다 더 무서운 놈이구나!’
The tiger thought: "What kind of fellow is this 'dried persimmon' that makes them stop crying immediately? Dried persimmon must be a fellow scarier than me!"

그런데 그때, 엄마가 방문을 열었어요. 호랑이는 깜짝 놀라 외양간 안으로 도망갔어요.
But just then, the mother opened the room door. The tiger was startled and fled into the cowshed.

외양간 안에 숨어 있던 호랑이는 누군가 외양간으로 살금살금 들어오는 것을 봤어요.
While hiding in the cowshed, the tiger saw someone creeping into the shed.

‘저게 곶감이구나!’ 호랑이는 생각했어요.
"That must be the dried persimmon!" the tiger thought.

그런데 사실 외양간 안으로 들어온 사람은 소를 훔치러 온 소도둑이었어요.
But actually, the person who entered the cowshed was a cattle thief who came to steal a cow.

호랑이는 그것도 모르고 무서워서 벌벌 떨었어요. 그리고 소 옆에 몸을 엎드리고 숨었어요.
Not knowing this, the tiger trembled with fear. And he lay down next to the cow and hid.

바로 그때, 천천히 다가온 소도둑이 호랑이 머리를 더듬었어요.
Right then, the cattle thief, approaching slowly, felt around the tiger's head.

호랑이는 깜짝 놀라 벌떡 일어났어요. 
The tiger was startled and jumped up.

그런데 벌떡 일어난 호랑이 때문에 소도둑이 깜짝 놀라서 호랑이 등에 올라타 버렸어요.
But because of the tiger jumping up, the cattle thief was so startled that he ended up jumping onto the tiger's back.

“아이구, 내 등에 곶감이 탔잖아!”
"Oh no, a dried persimmon has climbed onto my back!"

호랑이는 등에 곶감이라는 무서운 놈이 탄 줄 알고 힘껏 뛰기 시작했어요.
The tiger, thinking the scary fellow called "dried persimmon" was on his back, began to run with all his might.

소도둑도 호랑이 등에서 떨어지지 않으려고 호랑이에게 힘껏 매달렸어요.
The cattle thief also clung to the tiger with all his strength so as not to fall off.

호랑이는 생각했어요. ‘이상하다, 곶감이 왜 안 떨어지지? 정말 무서운 놈이야.’
The tiger thought: "That's strange, why won't the dried persimmon fall off? He's a truly scary fellow."

호랑이 등에 매달려 가던 소도둑은 소가 너무 빠른 것이 이상하다고 생각했어요.
The cattle thief, riding on the tiger's back, thought it was strange that the "cow" was so fast.

‘이상하다, 소가 왜 이렇게 빠르지?’
"That's strange, why is this cow so fast?"

어느새 날이 밝았어요. 그제야 소도둑은 자신이 호랑이를 타고 있다는 것을 알게 되었어요.
Before long, day broke. Only then did the cattle thief realize he was riding a tiger.

“세상에! 내가 지금 호랑이 등에 올라타 있잖아!”
"Good heavens! I'm sitting on a tiger's back right now!"

소도둑은 너무 무서웠어요. 그런데 그때, 커다란 나무가 보였어요.
The cattle thief was terrified. But just then, he saw a large tree.

소도둑은 재빨리 뛰어서 나무에 매달렸어요. 그리고 소도둑은 호랑이를 피해 멀리 도망갔어요.
The cattle thief quickly jumped and clung to the tree. And the cattle thief ran far away to escape the tiger.

“아이구, 호랑이한테 잡아먹힐 뻔했네.”
"Phew, I almost got eaten by a tiger."

호랑이도 곶감이 등에서 떨어진 줄 알고 더 빨리 뛰어 도망갔어요.
The tiger also thought the dried persimmon had fallen off his back and ran away even faster.

“아이구, 곶감한테 잡아먹힐 뻔했네.”
"Phew, I almost got eaten by a dried persimmon."

그 후에 호랑이와 소도둑은 다시는 마을로 오지 않았다고 해요.
They say that after that, the tiger and the cattle thief never came back to the village.

호랑이는 곶감이, 소도둑은 호랑이가 너무 무서웠거든요.
Because the tiger was so afraid of the dried persimmon, and the cattle thief was so afraid of the tiger.
